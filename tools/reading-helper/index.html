How to write Leveled Text

Level A (Emergent):

Use 1 simple sentence per page. Stick to CVC words (cat, dog, sit) and basic sight words (the, a, see, is, on).

Level B (Emergent):

Use 1-2 sentences per page. Introduce simple repetitive sentence structures (Sam likes to run. Sam likes to play.)

Level C (Early):

Use 2-3 sentences per page. Use more varied sentence styles, punctuation, and blends (tree, bird, green, nest).

Level D (Early):

Use 3-4 sentences per page. Introduce basic story lines, dialogues, compound words, and a wider vocabulary (sandcastle, watermelon).

Phonics-First Learning

By breaking words into onsets (starting consonant sounds like c- or ch-) and rimes (vowel/ending combinations like -at or -air), early readers decode words naturally rather than relying on sight-memorization alone.

Bimodal Reading (TTS)

Our read-aloud features highlight words in real-time as they are spoken. Research shows that seeing and hearing text simultaneously (bimodal presentation) improves vocabulary, tracking, and reading speed.

Inclusive Accessibility

Choose from warm sepia themes, high-contrast layouts, text sizing, and dyslexia-friendly typography to provide a comfortable, stress-free reading environment tailored to your learner's needs.

About Leveled Reading Helper

The Leveled Reading Helper is a free educational tool designed to support preschool, kindergarten, and elementary school students who are learning to read. It aligns with science of reading principles by offering controlled vocabulary text, phonic-based auditory feedback, and visual tracking support.

All processing happens directly in the browser—your text and stories are never saved to our servers, keeping your student's learning session private and secure.
